⚙️ Room Configuration & Settings

What does the "Budget" setting mean? ▼

Budget is the total virtual money each team has to bid on players. Default is ₹2000L (200 million), matching official IPL format. Once a team's budget runs out, they can't bid anymore. Higher budgets mean more flexibility; lower budgets mean tighter constraints and faster auctions. Choose based on your preference for casual vs intense gameplay.

What is the "Squad Size" setting? ▼

Squad size is the maximum number of players each team can buy in the auction. Default is 25 players. Smaller squads (15-18) mean shorter, faster auctions with limited bench strength. Larger squads (25+) allow deeper rosters with more flexibility. Choose based on how long you want the auction to last.

What does "Bid Timer" do? ▼

The bid timer sets how long each player is available for bidding in seconds. Default is 30 seconds. During this time, teams can make multiple bids on the player. When time expires, the highest bidder wins the player. Shorter timers (10-15s) create fast-paced, intense auctions. Longer timers (45-60s) allow more strategic thinking.

What's the difference between "Random" and "By Category" player order? ▼

Random Order: Players come up in unpredictable sequence. Nobody can prepare in advance. Creates exciting, unpredictable auctions with last-minute surprises.

By Category: All batsmen come first, then bowlers, then all-rounders. Allows strategic planning. You know which types of players are coming next and can prepare bidding strategy accordingly.

Can I make an auction private or password-protected? ▼

Yes! When creating an auction, you can optionally set a Passcode. When someone tries to join your room, they'll need to enter this passcode. This ensures only your invited friends can join. Share the room code and passcode only with people you trust. Passcodes are optional—leave it blank for public auctions.

💰 Bidding & Gameplay

How does bidding work during an auction? ▼

Bidding works as follows:

  • A player is displayed on screen with a starting base price
  • All teams can bid, increasing the price
  • Each team can place multiple bids on the same player
  • Bids must be higher than the previous bid
  • When the timer runs out, the highest bidder wins the player
  • The winning team's budget is deducted by the final bid amount
  • The process repeats for the next player
Can I see other teams' budgets and squad composition during the auction? ▼

Yes! During the auction, you can see:

  • Each team's remaining available budget
  • Number of players each team has acquired
  • Current highest bid on the active player
  • Which team made the last bid
  • Remaining time on the bid timer

This information helps you make informed decisions about whether to continue bidding or yield.

What happens if I run out of budget? ▼

Once your team's budget reaches zero, you can no longer bid on players. You'll be locked out of remaining auction rounds. Other teams continue bidding until all players are sold or the maximum squad size is reached per team. Running out of budget early is a strategic risk—manage it carefully!

Can I bid on a player my team doesn't need? ▼

Yes, though it's not strategically wise. Sometimes players bid on players they don't need to:

  • Block competitors: Drive up prices so rivals spend more budget
  • Test strategy: Experiment with bidding tactics
  • Fun factor: Create excitement and unpredictability

However, wasting budget on unwanted players is risky. You might miss crucial players you actually need later!

Is it possible to edit a bid I've already placed? ▼

No, bids are final once placed. You cannot edit or retract a bid. Each bid incrementally increases the player's price. This is why it's important to think before bidding—make sure you really want the player at that price point!

🎯 Strategy & Tips

What's a good budget allocation strategy? ▼

A balanced approach typically works best:

  • 40% (~₹800L): Premium star players (captains, match-winners)
  • 35% (~₹700L): Mid-tier quality players (consistent performers)
  • 15% (~₹300L): Support players (role specialists, bench strength)
  • 10% (~₹200L): Reserve fund (emergencies, last-minute pickups)

This prevents overspending on superstars while ensuring squad depth and flexibility.

How can I win the auction? ▼

Success requires multiple factors:

  • Smart planning: Know which players you want before auction starts
  • Budget discipline: Don't overspend early; maintain flexibility
  • Market awareness: Track average prices and adjust strategy
  • Selective bidding: Bid on fewer players but win at good prices
  • Analysis: Evaluate player form and team needs thoroughly
  • Boldness: Take calculated risks and capitalize on opportunities
Should I bid aggressively or conservatively? ▼

Both approaches have merits:

Aggressive Strategy: Bid high early to secure star players. Risk: runs out of budget; misses late-stage opportunities.

Conservative Strategy: Bid selectively and preserve budget. Advantage: flexibility; can exploit late opportunities.

Hybrid Approach (Recommended): Bid aggressively on 2-3 must-have players, then switch to conservative bidding. Balances quality with flexibility.

What are the most valuable player roles typically? ▼

Value depends on auction dynamics, but generally:

  • All-rounders: Highest value (batting + bowling = dual utility)
  • Star opening batsmen: High value (consistent run machines)
  • Death-over bowlers: High value (crucial in T20 finishes)
  • Wicket-keepers: Medium value (specific slots, but essential)
  • Support players: Lower value (replaceable bench options)
How do I analyze if a bid price is fair? ▼

Use these methods to evaluate player value:

  • Compare similar players: What did comparable players fetch?
  • Track average prices: Monitor prices across the auction
  • Player form: Recent performance (last 5-10 matches, recent tournaments)
  • Team needs: Does your team urgently need this position?
  • Alternative availability: How many similar players remain unsold?
  • Bid momentum: Is price inflating due to competition or value?

🎯 Advanced Strategies & Tips

What's the optimal budget allocation strategy? ▼

Classic budget allocation for ₹2000L total budget:

  • Batsmen (6-7 players): ₹800L (40%) - Focus on quality openers and middle-order
  • Bowlers (4-5 players): ₹600L (30%) - Mix of pacers and spinners
  • All-rounders (3-4 players): ₹400L (20%) - Dual-utility value
  • Reserve (2-3 players): ₹200L (10%) - Bench strength and flexibility

Adjust percentages based on your auction's player availability and competitor strategies.

How do I identify undervalued players? ▼

Champions find value where others don't. Look for:

  • Recent form improvers: Players performing well recently but not hot names
  • Returning from injury: Players coming back might be priced low despite quality
  • Inconsistent performers: Had one bad season but historically strong
  • Role specialists: Excel in niche positions that fill your specific needs
  • Young emerging talent: Potential superstars not yet recognized
  • Overlooked by competitors: Players others aren't bidding for due to lack of awareness
How do I bid strategically when multiple teams want same player? ▼

When facing bidding wars:

  • Early assessment: Can you afford to win? Is it worth it?
  • Incremental bidding: Small increases put pressure on competitors
  • Large jump bids: Surprise high jump bid often causes opponents to fold
  • Psychological tactics: Hesitation signals, sudden aggression, patterns all matter
  • Know your walk-away price: Set maximum bid beforehand and stick to it
  • Read competitors: Desperate players overpay; disciplined players back out early
What's the benefit of using watchlist strategically? ▼

Strategic watchlist usage:

  • Primary targets: Must-have players you'll pursue aggressively
  • Backup options: Similar-skill players to bid on if primaries overprice
  • Competitor tracking: Star players competitors will likely bid for
  • Value sleepers: Undervalued players you've identified as steals
  • Category fills: Specific role players (openers, death bowlers, etc.) you need

Works best when you have 20-30 watchlist players across all categories.

How should I adjust strategy based on auction size (budget/teams)? ▼

Small Budget (₹500-1000L): Highly selective. Bid on absolute must-haves. Pass on most players.

Standard Budget (₹2000L): Balanced approach. Mix quality purchases with value picks.

Large Budget (₹3000L+): Can afford multiple premium players. Emphasis on depth over selectivity.

Fewer teams (2-3): Less competition. Can wait longer to bid. Better chance of value picks.

More teams (8): Higher competition. Bid earlier and faster. Premium players sell quickly at high prices.

What are common auction psychology mistakes? ▼

Avoid these mental pitfalls:

  • Auction fever: Getting caught up in bidding wars and overpaying
  • Sunk cost fallacy: Continuing to bid because you already invested in a player
  • Anchoring bias: First bid becomes reference point; bid higher consequently
  • FOMO (Fear of Missing Out): Bidding on players you don't actually want
  • Herd mentality: Everyone bidding on same stars instead of exploring value
  • Emotional attachment: Chasing favorite players regardless of budget impact

⚙️ Auction Mechanics & Rules

How is base bid price determined for each player? ▼

Base bid prices are set algorithmically based on:

  • Player performance rating (historical stats)
  • Current form and recent matches
  • Player role and category
  • International vs domestic status
  • Experience level (star vs emerging player)

This ensures fair starting points while allowing market forces (competitive bidding) to determine final prices.

What happens if two teams try to bid at exactly same time? ▼

Our real-time system processes bids with millisecond precision. If two bids arrive simultaneously:

  • The system timestamp determines the order
  • Slightly earlier bid wins priority
  • Later bid is rejected and can be re-placed
  • You'll see clear feedback on which bid won

Happens rarely due to millisecond timing. Internet speed and device timing affect this outcome.

Can a team bid on the same player multiple times? ▼

Yes! Teams can place multiple bids on the same player throughout the auction round, but only the highest bid counts. Each new bid must be higher than the previous highest bid for that player. Once timer expires, the highest bidding team wins that player.

What happens if a player doesn't sell (no bids)? ▼

If a player receives zero bids when the timer expires, the player remains unsold and moves to the next auction round. The player may come up again later, or the auction might end if all remaining players are bypassed by all teams.
